Effortlessly manage your unpublished drafts with periodic notifications and customizable email reminders.

== Description ==

The **WPApps Draft Notifier** plugin offers a hassle-free way to keep track of your forgotten or outdated drafts in WordPress. It automatically sends email notifications to site admins, authors, or both, making sure that no unpublished content is left behind. 

Whether you are managing a small blog or a large content-heavy site, this plugin helps you maintain a smooth editorial workflow by staying on top of your drafts.

**Key Features:**

- Automatic email notifications about old drafts
- Customizable email templates with a built-in WYSIWYG editor
- Set notification recipients to administrators, authors, or both
- Configurable draft age threshold before notifications are triggered
- Compatible with all post types, including custom post types

Ideal for bloggers, business owners, or content managers who need to stay organized and informed about their unpublished content.

== Installation ==

1. Upload the `wpapps-draft-notifier` folder to the `/wp-content/plugins/` directory.
2. Activate the plugin through the 'Plugins' menu in WordPress.
3. Go to the WPApps Draft Notifier settings page to configure your preferences.

== Frequently Asked Questions ==

= 1. Why should I use WPApps Draft Notifier? =

This plugin helps you avoid leaving your content incomplete or forgotten. By sending reminders about old drafts, you can keep your publishing workflow active and timely.

= 2. How do I configure the email notifications? =

After activating the plugin, navigate to the WPApps Draft Notifier settings page where you can adjust the email templates, select recipients, and configure how old drafts need to be before a notification is triggered.

= 3. Can I customize the notification email? =

Yes! You can easily customize the email template using the built-in WYSIWYG editor. Insert placeholders like `[drafts_list]` to dynamically populate the list of drafts in the notification.

= 4. Who receives the notifications? =

You can choose whether notifications go to the site administrator, draft authors, or both, by configuring the options on the settings page.

= 5. Does this plugin support custom post types? =

Yes, WPApps Draft Notifier works with all post types, including custom post types.
